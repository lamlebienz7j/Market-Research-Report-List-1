<p><p>The glycerol esters market comprises three main types: monoglycerides, diglycerides, and triglycerides. Monoglycerides consist of one fatty acid esterified to glycerol and are commonly used as emulsifiers in food and cosmetics. Diglycerides, containing two fatty acids, serve similar functions, enhancing texture and stability in products. Triglycerides, the most prevalent form, consist of three fatty acids and are primarily utilized for energy storage in organisms and as constituents in various food and cosmetic formulations, influencing texture and flavor.</p></p>
