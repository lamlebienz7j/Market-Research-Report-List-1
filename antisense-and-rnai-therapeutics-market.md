<p><p>The Antisense and RNAi therapeutics market comprises two primary types: RNA interference (RNAi) and antisense RNA therapies. RNAi involves using small interfering RNA (siRNA) to silence specific genes, effectively reducing the expression of harmful proteins. Antisense RNA therapies utilize designed nucleic acid sequences to bind complementary mRNA, blocking translation and preventing the production of target proteins. Both approaches are pivotal in treating genetic disorders and various diseases, showcasing advanced methods to manipulate gene expression for therapeutic benefits.</p></p>
