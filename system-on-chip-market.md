<p><p>The System-on-Chip (SoC) market is categorized into three primary types: digital, analog, and mixed-signal. Digital SoCs focus on processing and managing digital data, commonly used in computers and smartphones. Analog SoCs are designed for processing real-world signals, essential in applications like audio and video processing. Mixed-signal SoCs combine both digital and analog functionalities, enabling efficient processing of varied signal types in a single chip. This versatility facilitates advanced applications across consumer electronics, automotive, and IoT devices.</p></p>
