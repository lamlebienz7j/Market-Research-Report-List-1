<p><p>The whole milk powder market is primarily divided into two types: regular and instant. Regular whole milk powder is produced by removing moisture from whole milk, resulting in a product that requires rehydration before use. It is commonly used in baking, cooking, and dairy processing. Instant whole milk powder, on the other hand, dissolves quickly in water without clumping, making it favorable for applications where ease of mixing is essential, such as beverage formulations and convenience foods. Both types cater to diverse consumer needs and applications.</p></p>
